# Product Guidelines

## Content & Voice
- **Tone:** Professional and Authoritative. Content must be clear, direct, and formal. Avoid slang or overly casual language.
- **Goal:** To convey deep expertise, reliability, and to build a strong foundation of trust with both customers and vendors.

## Design Principles
- **Clarity and Hierarchy:** Information architecture must be intuitive. Use size, weight, and placement to guide users toward primary actions (e.g., "Add to Cart," "Complete Purchase").
- **Accessibility First:** The platform will be built to WCAG 2.1 standards. This includes high color contrast, full keyboard navigability, and semantic HTML for screen readers.
- **Consistency Above All:** A centralized design system will govern all components. Every interaction should feel familiar and predictable across all services and platforms.

## User Feedback & Error Handling
- **Informative and Action-Oriented:** Errors must never be cryptic. Explain what happened and provide a clear, one-click path to a solution.
- **Subtle and Non-Intrusive:** Use toast notifications for success states and minor updates to avoid breaking the user's flow.
- **Human-Centric Support:** Escalation to support staff must be visible and accessible from any point in the user journey.

## Visual Assets
- **High-Definition and Standardized:** Core product listings require high-resolution imagery with consistent aspect ratios and clean backgrounds to maintain a premium feel.
- **Authentic and Varied:** Lifestyle imagery is encouraged for brand storytelling and vendor storefronts to provide an organic, marketplace atmosphere.

## Performance & Reliability
- **Instantaneous Feel:** Sub-second First Contentful Paint (FCP) and low Time to Interactive (TTI) are hard requirements for a high-end experience.
- **Transparent Reliability:** Every background process must have a corresponding UI state. Users should always know that their data is being saved or their order is being processed.
